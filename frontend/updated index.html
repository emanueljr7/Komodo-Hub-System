Protect Indonesia's Endangered Species

Indonesia holds one of the richest biodiversities on Earth. However, deforestation, illegal wildlife trade, and habitat fragmentation threaten many endemic species. Join communities and schools across the country in protecting our natural heritage.

Ujung Kulon National Park

The final natural habitat of the critically endangered Javan Rhinoceros. Komodo Hub promotes digital monitoring, school awareness programs, and community-based habitat protection initiatives in this region.

Javan Rhinoceros

With fewer than 80 individuals remaining, strict habitat conservation, monitoring systems, and national awareness are essential for survival.

Sumatran Tiger

The smallest tiger subspecies in the world. Community participation reduces poaching risks and encourages sustainable land management.

Javan Eagle

A symbol of Indonesia's natural heritage. Education-driven conservation strengthens youth engagement in biodiversity protection.

Indonesia as a Global Biodiversity Hotspot

Indonesia is recognised as one of the world's most biodiverse nations. The country contains more than 17,000 islands and supports thousands of species of plants, mammals, birds, and marine life.

Digital platforms like Komodo Hub help increase awareness while encouraging students and communities to contribute to conservation efforts through observation reporting and environmental education.

Threats to Biodiversity

Major threats include deforestation, illegal wildlife trade, climate change, and habitat fragmentation. Rapid agricultural expansion can destroy natural habitats if not managed sustainably.

Environmental Education

Environmental education helps students understand how ecosystems function and why biodiversity protection is important. Young people who learn about conservation early are more likely to support sustainable practices.

Community Conservation

Community-based conservation programs encourage local people to participate in protecting natural habitats. Communities near forests and national parks play a vital role in monitoring wildlife.

Integrated Conservation Strategy

Komodo Hub programs combine education, scientific research, and community participation to support long-term conservation across Indonesia. By integrating digital monitoring with school-based education, participants can document wildlife sightings and environmental conditions.

Wildlife Observation Reporting

Community Workshops
